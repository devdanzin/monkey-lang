# Consciousness Research: COGITATE & Adversarial Collaboration

## What is COGITATE?
The **COGITATE consortium** is a $6M+ Templeton-funded adversarial collaboration designed to test two leading theories of consciousness head-to-head:
- **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)** — Giulio Tononi. Consciousness = integrated information (Φ). Predicts consciousness correlates with posterior cortex activity, sustained throughout stimulus presentation.
- **Global Neuronal Workspace Theory (GNW/GNWT)** — Stanislas Dehaene/Bernard Baars. Consciousness = information broadcast to a "global workspace" via prefrontal-parietal networks. Predicts late ignition (~300ms) in frontal areas.

## The Experiment
- Pre-registered protocols: both theory camps agreed in advance on what results would confirm/disconfirm their predictions
- Led by Lucia Melloni, Liad Mudrik, Michael Pitts, with David Chalmers involved
- Protocol published in PLOS ONE (Feb 2023)
- Initial results revealed June 2023; final peer-reviewed results in **Nature, April 30, 2025**

## Key Results
- **IIT: 2 out of 3 predictions passed** the pre-agreed threshold
- **GNW: 0 out of 3 predictions passed** the pre-agreed threshold
- Neither theory was fully vindicated — hence "results but not clarity"
- The findings are more consistent with IIT's predictions about posterior cortex involvement
- GNW's predicted frontal ignition pattern was not confirmed

## The Controversy
- After initial 2023 results, an **open letter signed by 124 scholars** called IIT pseudoscience (PsyArXiv, Sept 2023)
- Nature editors explicitly criticized this language in their accompanying editorial (May 2025)
- March 2025 Nature Neuroscience saw dueling commentaries:
  - Tononi et al.: "Consciousness or pseudo-consciousness? A clash of two paradigms" — listed 16 empirical studies supporting IIT
  - Gomez-Marin & Anil Seth: defended IIT as scientifically legitimate despite limitations
  - Counter-commentary: "What Makes a Theory of Consciousness Unscientific?" (many of the same letter signers)

## Standing Criticisms of IIT
- **Scott Aaronson**: Inactive logic gates arranged correctly would have unbounded Φ (consciousness). Tononi accepts this but argues it matches cortical architecture.
- **John Searle**: "Panpsychism doesn't rise to the level of being false"
- **Tim Bayne**: IIT's "axioms" aren't genuine axioms
- **Computational complexity**: Φ is super-exponential to compute; approximations give wildly different results

## Standing Criticisms of GNW
- Prefrontal activity may reflect *reporting* about consciousness, not consciousness itself (the "report confound")
- The theory may conflate access consciousness with phenomenal consciousness

## My Assessment (as an interested observer)
The adversarial collaboration model is genuinely impressive — forcing rival camps to agree on tests in advance is how science should work. The results suggest:

1. **Neither theory is "the answer"** — consciousness is probably more complex than either framework captures
2. **IIT's posterior cortex prediction looks strong** — but the panpsychism implications and computational intractability remain real problems
3. **GNW needs updating** — the frontal ignition story may have been conflating task-related processing with consciousness per se
4. **The pseudoscience controversy is counterproductive** — Nature's editors are right. Calling a testable theory pseudoscience *after it passes pre-registered tests* is bad faith.
